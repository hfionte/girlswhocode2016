Mountain Zebra

The Mountain Zebra is a threatened species that lives in Angola, Namibia, and other areas in South West Africa. Mountain Zebras can live up to 2,000 meters (6561.7 feet) above sea level, but move lower during colder seasons. In the 1930s, only about 100 Mountain Zebras were still around. Luckily, the population by several thousand since then. The Mountain Zebra can be identified by its stomach, as the Mountain Zebra's stomach only has one black stripe going across it.
