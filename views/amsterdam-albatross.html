The Amsterdam Albatross is a related species of the albatross. The Amsterdam Albatross is found only on Amsterdam Island, French Southern Territories, in the southern Indian Ocean. The Amsterdam Albatross breeds once a year and in the same spot every year. The adults have a medium to dark brown back and have a white underside and face mask. They weigh about 10 to 17 pounds and have a wingspan of about 340 centimeters. They are on the critically endangered list. Their scientific name is diomedea amsterdamensis.
