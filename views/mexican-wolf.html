About The Mexican Wolf

The Mexican Wolf is the most endangered wolf on the planet.The Mexican wolf preferred ponderosa pine-covered mountains, piñon-juniper forests, oak woodlands, and adjacent grasslands above 4,000 feet in elevation all very specific environments. After years of losing its natural habitat and deliberate extermination by settlers, the Mexican grey wolf was saved from extinction when it was placed on the Endangered Species List in 1976. Now there are only about 350 Mexican Wolves left. Mexican wolves are mainly found in the grasslands and forests of New Mexico and Arizona.
